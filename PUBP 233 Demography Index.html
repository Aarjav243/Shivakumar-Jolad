Course Description

This introductory course on demography focuses on measures of population, theories of population change, and the social and economic processes that influence it. The formal demography component introduces measures of mortality, fertility, and migration, and examines how these shape population size, composition, and distribution.

Course at a Glance

  • Code: PUBP 233
  • Level: 200 (Undergraduate)
  • Area: Public Policy
  • L:T:P: 3:0:0 (45 Contact hrs)

Objectives

  • Introduce the scientific study of human populations
  • Study population size, age structure, and spatial distribution
  • Understand and measure mortality, fertility, and migration
  • Compute demographic measures and project population change
  • Apply demographic methods to mortality and disease morbidity
  • Critically examine the consequences of population change
