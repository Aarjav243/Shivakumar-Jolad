About the Course

This introductory course on demography focuses on measures of population, theories of population change, and the social and economic processes that influence it. The formal demography component introduces measures of mortality, fertility, and migration, and examines how these shape population size, composition, and distribution. Students learn to construct life tables and calculate public-health measures such as infant mortality and life expectancy. The course also examines forces and models of migration and urbanization, and the social and economic theories of fertility change.

Through hands-on exercises, students project populations and analyse changes in age and sex composition. The social demography component covers classical population theories, demographic transition theory, and the politics of population — including debates on population growth and development, and implications for labour markets and education.

Objectives

  • Introduce the scientific study of human populations and address common myths about population
  • Study population size, age structure, and spatial distribution
  • Understand and measure the drivers of population change — mortality, fertility, and migration
  • Compute demographic measures and project population change using public data
  • Apply demographic methods to mortality and disease morbidity in public health
  • Critically examine the consequences of population change for society, politics, and the economy
