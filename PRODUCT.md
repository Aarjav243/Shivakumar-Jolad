# Product

## Register

brand

> Note: Hero, About, and Media sections are brand surfaces (design IS the product). Research, Publications, and Teaching sections carry product-like density — information architecture matters as much as aesthetics there.

## Users

Academics, researchers, policymakers, graduate students, institutional search committees, journalists, and prospective collaborators. They arrive with a specific credibility question: "Is this person worth engaging with?" They scan hierarchy before reading prose. They may return for a specific paper or course syllabus.

## Product Purpose

Digital professional presence for Shivakumar Jolad, Associate Professor of Public Policy at FLAME University. Establishes scholarly authority, surfaces research and publications, and makes it easy for the right people to find, assess, and contact him. Success looks like a visitor leaving with a confident sense of his expertise and reach.

## Brand Personality

Scholarly, Authoritative, Timeless

## Anti-references

- Generic university pages: plain institutional HTML, unstructured lists, blue hyperlinks, no visual hierarchy, gray backgrounds, Times New Roman
- Nothing that could be mistaken for a department faculty directory or a WordPress academic theme

## Design Principles

1. **Gravity before glamour.** Every visual choice must earn trust before it earns attention. Ornament that doesn't signal quality should not exist.
2. **Hierarchy that teaches.** The layout should lead a first-time visitor to understand what matters — without them having to decide where to look.
3. **Density with dignity.** Publications and research sections carry significant information load. Maintain readability and breathing room even at high content density.
4. **Restrained ornamentation.** The gold accent exists to signal quality and guide the eye — not to decorate. Use it sparingly and purposefully.
5. **Timeless over trendy.** Design choices should look correct in ten years. Avoid anything that dates to a particular design moment.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Existing codebase includes skip links, semantic HTML, ARIA labels, and keyboard-navigable focus states. Reduced-motion support is a consideration given the GSAP animation load.
