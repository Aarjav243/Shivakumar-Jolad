Course Description

This course introduces the rudiments of data analysis for students of social science. It incorporates elementary but important methods for gathering and analysing data to answer questions of social, economic, cultural, political, and policy interest, giving students the skills to readily understand and analyse quantitative data.

Learning Objectives

  • Develop an intuition for data and extract meaning from it
  • Analyse and visualise data and make meaningful inferences
  • Learn probability, descriptive statistics, and statistical inference
  • Work with sampling distributions, estimation and hypothesis testing
  • Critically examine statistical results through case studies
  • Gain hands-on skills with statistical software

Core Books & Reading

  • Krieg – Statistics and Data Analysis for Social Science
  • Hesse & Ofosu – Statistical Methods for the Social Sciences
  • Hans Rosling – Factfulness
  • Rukmini S. – Whole Numbers and Half Truths
  • Edward Tufte – The Visual Display of Quantitative Information
  • DeVeaux & Velleman – Introduction to Stats, 3rd ed.
  • M. Triola – Elementary Statistics, 11th ed.
