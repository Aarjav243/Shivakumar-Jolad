Course Description

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to India's education system, its historical development from pre-colonial times through post-independence reforms, and the major policy frameworks that shape schooling and higher education today. Students engage with debates on access, equity, curriculum, governance, and evidence-based interventions.

Course Structure

  • Part 1: Education System (Modules 1–4, ~20 hrs)
  • Part 2: Education Bureaucracy & Finance (Module 6, ~10 hrs)
  • Part 3: Curriculum, Pedagogy & Learning (Module 7, ~9 hrs)
  • Part 4: Education Interventions & Reforms (Module 8, ~4 hrs)

Core Texts

  • Sarangapani – Constructing School Knowledge
  • Naik & Nurullah – History of Education in India
  • Ghosh – Development of Education in India
  • Kothari Commission Report (1964–66)
  • Mangla – Education in India
  • NPE 1986 & POA 1992
  • Draft NEP 2019
  • Muralidharan – Accelerating India's Development

Assessment

As per course outline shared separately. Students are expected to complete assigned readings before each session and participate actively in seminars.
